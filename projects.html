Gross Domestic Happiness

(In progress)

Interactive set of visualizations for data from the 2018 World Happiness Report, which tracks countries' national happiness for that year. Features an interactive bump chart showing how countries' happiness rankings have changed over time and an interactive map that allows you to view countries' specific values and also scroll to countries in the bump chart by clicking on the country in the map.

Project was implemented primarily in JavaScript with some data manipulation done in R. The bump chart was made using d3.js and the map was made using leaflet.js,turf.js

What Drives US Counterterrorism Spending?

This capstone research project looks at the relationship between the levels of terrorism and spending on counterterrorism by the United States before and after 9/11. We did an overview of how the process of counterterrorism policymaking and the policies themselves have evolved since the 20th century. We then ran a series of regression models to determine if levels of terrorism domestically and by designated Foreign Terrorist Organizations abroad had a significant impact on changes in spending.

Overall, we found that levels of terrorism, either domestic or international, were not a significant predictor of changes in spending. We also found that 9/11 did not have any impact on this relationship.

Contextualizing the Rally 'Round the Flag Effect

This research project evaluates the evidence for the rally 'round the flag effect and its logical derivative, the diversionary use of force, which is a popular theory for explaining when leaders go to war. With a series of regression models comparing United States presidential approval ratings with uses of force over time.

Overall, I found little statistical evidence for the rally ' round the flag effect; uses of force did not create a consistent bump in the president's approval ratings.
